<div class="gl-term" id="applicant">
<h3>Applicant</h3>
<p>The individual or business applying for a surety bond. Also called the <strong>principal</strong> once the bond is issued.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="bond-amount">
<h3>Bond Amount (Penal Sum)</h3>
<p>The maximum dollar amount the surety may be required to pay if a valid claim is filed.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="bond-claim">
<h3>Bond Claim</h3>
<p>A formal allegation that the bonded party violated laws, regulations, or contractual obligations. If valid, the surety pays the harmed party and the principal must reimburse the surety.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="bond-form">
<h3>Bond Form</h3>
<p>The official document issued by the state or obligee that outlines the bond's requirements, obligations, and conditions.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="bond-premium">
<h3>Bond Premium</h3>
<p>The cost the principal pays to obtain the bond. Typically a small percentage of the total bond amount.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="commercial-bond">
<h3>Commercial Bond</h3>
<p>A category of bonds required by government agencies to ensure compliance with laws and regulations (e.g., <a href="/license-permit-bond-hub/">License &amp; Permit Bonds</a>).</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="contract-bond">
<h3>Contract Bond</h3>
<p>A bond used in construction to guarantee performance, payment, or bid obligations. See <a href="/payment-and-performance-bonds/">Payment &amp; Performance Bonds</a>.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="credit-check">
<h3>Credit Check</h3>
<p>A review of the applicant's credit history used by the surety to determine risk and pricing.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="indemnity-agreement">
<h3>Indemnity Agreement</h3>
<p>A legally binding contract requiring the principal (and often owners) to reimburse the surety for any losses or claims paid.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="license-permit-bond">
<h3>License &amp; Permit Bond</h3>
<p>A bond required by state or local agencies to ensure businesses follow regulations and operate ethically. See the <a href="/license-permit-bond-hub/">License &amp; Permit Bond Hub</a>.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="obligee">
<h3>Obligee</h3>
<p>The government agency or entity requiring the bond. The obligee is protected by the bond.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="performance-bond">
<h3>Performance Bond</h3>
<p>A contract bond guaranteeing that a contractor will complete a project according to the contract terms. See <a href="/payment-and-performance-bonds/">Payment &amp; Performance Bonds</a>.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="payment-bond">
<h3>Payment Bond</h3>
<p>A contract bond guaranteeing that subcontractors, suppliers, and laborers will be paid. See <a href="/payment-and-performance-bonds/">Payment &amp; Performance Bonds</a>.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="principal">
<h3>Principal</h3>
<p>The individual or business required to obtain the bond and responsible for fulfilling the bonded obligations.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="renewal-term">
<h3>Renewal Term</h3>
<p>The period for which a bond remains active before it must be renewed, often annually.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="surety">
<h3>Surety</h3>
<p>The company that issues the bond and guarantees the principal's obligations. If a claim is paid, the principal must reimburse the surety.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="underwriting">
<h3>Underwriting</h3>
<p>The evaluation process used by the surety to assess risk, review credit, and determine bond pricing.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="underwriter">
<h3>Underwriter</h3>
<p>The professional who reviews the application and decides whether the bond can be issued and at what rate.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="utility-bond">
<h3>Utility Bond</h3>
<p>A bond guaranteeing payment of utility bills for commercial accounts.</p>
</div>
<div class="gl-term" id="warranty-bond">
<h3>Warranty Bond</h3>
<p>A bond guaranteeing that a contractor will correct defects or workmanship issues during a warranty period. See <a href="/payment-and-performance-bonds/">Payment &amp; Performance Bonds</a>.</p>
</div>

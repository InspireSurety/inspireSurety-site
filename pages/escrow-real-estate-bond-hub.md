What Are Escrow \& Real Estate Bonds?



Escrow and real estate bonds are financial guarantees required by state licensing agencies to ensure:



Proper handling of client funds

Ethical business practices

Compliance with state real estate and escrow laws

Accurate documentation and record‑keeping

Protection of consumers from fraud or mismanagement

These bonds apply to a wide range of real estate and financial professionals.



Core Types of Escrow \& Real Estate Bonds

Notary Bonds - Guarantee that notaries perform their duties ethically and in compliance with state law.



Mortgage Broker Bonds - Ensure mortgage brokers follow lending regulations and protect borrowers from misconduct.



Escrow Agent Bonds - Required for escrow companies to ensure proper handling of client funds and documents.



Title Agent Bonds - Guarantee compliance with title insurance and settlement regulations.



Real Estate Broker Bonds - Required in some states to ensure brokers follow real estate licensing laws.



Closing Agent / Settlement Agent Bonds - Protect consumers during real estate closings and settlement transactions.

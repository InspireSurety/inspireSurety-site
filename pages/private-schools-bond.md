What Is a Private School Bond?

A private school bond (also called an education provider bond, proprietary school bond, or tuition bond) is a state‑required surety bond that protects students and families when a private school collects tuition or fees.



This bond protects students from:



Loss of prepaid tuition

School closure or bankruptcy

Failure to deliver promised educational programs

Misrepresentation of courses or credentials

Violations of state education regulations

If you operate a private school or training program, your state may require this bond before issuing or renewing your license.



Private School Bond Amounts and Cost

Bond amounts vary by state and typically range from $10,000 to $250,000, depending on:



Tuition collected

Enrollment size

Type of educational programs offered

State consumer‑protection laws

Who Needs a Private School Bond

You may need a private school bond if you operate:



A private K‑12 school

A charter or independent academy

A vocational or trade school

A technical training program

A tutoring or enrichment center

A proprietary or for‑profit school

A continuing‑education or certification program

Any school collecting tuition or fees in advance may be required to file this bond.



Private School Bond Requirements \& Eligibility

States typically require:



Completed private school license application

State‑mandated bond amount

Credit review

Background check

Proof of curriculum and financial stability

Annual or biannual renewal

Some states require higher bond amounts for multi‑campus schools or large enrollment programs.

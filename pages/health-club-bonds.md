What Is a Health Club Bond?

A health club bond (also called a gym bond, fitness center bond, or prepaid membership bond) is a state‑required surety bond that protects consumers when a health club sells prepaid memberships or long‑term service contracts.



This bond protects members from:



Loss of prepaid membership fees

Club closure or bankruptcy

Misrepresentation of services

Failure to provide contracted fitness programs

Violations of state health‑club regulations

If you operate a gym or fitness facility that collects upfront payments, your state may require this bond before issuing or renewing your license.



Health Club Bond Amounts and Cost

Bond amounts vary by state and typically range from $10,000 to $300,000, depending on:

Who Needs a Health Club Bond?

You may need a health club bond if you operate:



A gym or fitness center

A boutique fitness studio

A martial arts or boxing studio

A yoga or Pilates studio

A wellness or training facility

A personal‑training center offering prepaid packages

Any business selling prepaid memberships or long‑term fitness contracts may be required to file this bond.



Health Club Bond Requirements \& Eligibility

States typically require:



Completed health club license application

State‑mandated bond amount

Credit review

Proof of financial responsibility

Annual or biannual renewal

Some states require higher bond amounts for multi‑location gyms or large membership bases.

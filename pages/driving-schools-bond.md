What Is a Driving School Bond?

A driving school bond (also called a driver training school bond or driving instructor bond) is a state‑required surety bond that guarantees driving schools operate ethically, follow licensing laws, and protect students from financial harm.



This bond protects the public from:



Misrepresentation of training services

Failure to provide paid instruction

Improper handling of tuition or fees

Violations of state driver‑education laws

Fraud or misconduct by instructors or staff

If you’re applying for or renewing a driving school license, your state may require this bond before granting approval.

Who Needs a Driving School Bond?

You may need a driving school bond if you operate:



A teen driver‑education school

An adult driving school

A commercial driver training program (CDL)

A behind‑the‑wheel instruction service

A defensive driving or traffic‑school program

A private driver‑training business

Any business providing paid driver‑education services may be required to file this bond.



Driving School Bond Requirements \& Eligibility

States typically require:



Completed driving school license application

State‑mandated bond amount

Credit review

Background check

Proof of curriculum and instructor qualifications

Annual or biannual renewal

Some states require separate bonds for branch locations or CDL training programs.

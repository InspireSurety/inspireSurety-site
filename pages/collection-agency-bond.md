What Is a Collection Agency Bond?

A collection agency bond is a state‑required surety bond that guarantees collection agencies operate ethically, follow all debt‑collection laws, and protect consumers from financial harm.



This bond protects the public and the state from:



Misrepresentation or abusive collection practices

Mishandling of client funds

Failure to remit collected payments

Violations of state or federal collection laws

Fraud or misconduct

If you’re applying for or renewing a collection agency license, your state requires this bond before issuing the license.



Collection Agency Bond Amount \& Cost

Bond amounts vary by state and typically range from $5,000 to $50,000, though some states require higher limits.

Who Needs a Collection Agency Bond?

You may need a collection agency bond if you are:



A third‑party debt collector

A debt buyer

A repossession company (in some states)

A loan servicing company

A skip‑tracing or recovery agency

A medical or financial collections firm

Any business collecting debts on behalf of others may be required to file this bond.



Collection Agency Bond Requirements and Eligibility

States typically require:



Completed collection agency license application

State‑mandated bond amount

Credit review

Background checks

Annual renewal

Some states require separate bonds for branch locations.

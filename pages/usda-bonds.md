What Are USDA Bonds?
USDA bonds are federal surety bonds required by the U.S. Department of Agriculture to ensure businesses operating in regulated agricultural industries comply with federal laws, protect producers, and safeguard the food supply chain.

These bonds protect farmers, suppliers, and consumers from:

Non‑payment for agricultural products
Misrepresentation of goods
Failure to meet federal grading or inspection standards
Violations of USDA program rules
Financial loss due to business misconduct
If your business operates under USDA oversight, you may be required to file one or more USDA‑mandated bonds before receiving or renewing your license.

Types of USDA Bonds We Provide
Below is a complete list of the most common USDA‑required bonds.

1. PACA Bonds (Perishable Agricultural Commodities Act) - Required for produce dealers, brokers, and wholesalers. Guarantees payment to growers and compliance with PACA regulations.

2. Grain Dealer Bonds - Required for grain buyers, handlers, and elevators. Protects producers from non‑payment for grain purchases.

3. Grain Warehouse Bonds (USDA Warehouse Act) - Required for public grain warehouses storing agricultural commodities. Ensures proper storage, handling, and delivery of stored grain.

4. Livestock Dealer Bonds (P&S Act Bonds) - Required under the Packers & Stockyards Act. Protects livestock sellers from non‑payment and unfair practices.

5. Market Agency Bonds - Required for livestock commission merchants and market agencies. Guarantees proper handling of livestock proceeds.

6. Packers Bonds - Required for meat packers purchasing livestock. Ensures timely payment to producers and compliance with USDA rules.

7. Cotton Warehouse Bonds - Required for cotton warehouses storing bales under USDA regulation. Guarantees proper storage and delivery of cotton.

8. Tobacco Warehouse & Dealer Bonds - Required for tobacco dealers and storage facilities. Ensures compliance with USDA grading, inspection, and payment rules.

9. Milk Handler / Dairy Bonds - Required for milk handlers, processors, and distributors. Protects dairy farmers from non‑payment for milk deliveries.

10. USDA Forest Service Timber Sale- These bonds protect the federal government by guaranteeing proper payment, responsible harvesting, environmental stewardship, and full site restoration.

Who Needs a USDA Bond
You may need a USDA bond if you operate:

A produce dealership
A grain elevator or grain buyer
A livestock auction, market agency, or packer
A cotton or tobacco warehouse
A dairy processing or distribution business
A commodity storage facility
Any business regulated under PACA, P&S Act, or the USDA Warehouse Act
If your business buys, sells, stores, or handles agricultural commodities, a USDA bond may be mandatory.

USDA Bond Requirements & Eligibility
USDA programs typically require:

Completed federal license application
USDA‑mandated bond amount
Credit review
Financial statements
Annual or semi‑annual renewal
Some USDA programs require increased bond amounts based on volume or risk.

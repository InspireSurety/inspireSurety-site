Federal \& BLM Bond Hub



Your Complete Resource for Federal, BLM \& Government‑Required Bonds

Federal and BLM bonds ensure businesses comply with U.S. government regulations, protect public lands, and meet financial obligations tied to federal programs. This hub organizes all your federal‑related bond content into one authoritative, easy‑to‑navigate resource.



What Are Federal \& BLM Bonds?



Federal and BLM bonds are financial guarantees required by U.S. government agencies to ensure:



Compliance with federal regulations

Protection of public lands and natural resources

Proper handling of funds, taxes, and fees

Completion of required improvements or restoration

Ethical and lawful business operations

These bonds apply to industries such as mining, oil \& gas, agriculture, housing, and international trade.



Core Types of Federal \& BLM Bonds

BLM Reclamation Bonds - Required by the Bureau of Land Management for mining, drilling, and surface disturbance on federal land.



BLM Right‑of‑Way (ROW) Bonds - Guarantee compliance for utilities, pipelines, and infrastructure projects crossing federal land.



BIA Lease Bonds (Bureau of Indian Affairs) - Required for businesses operating on tribal lands under federal oversight.



U.S. Customs Bonds - Guarantee compliance with import/export regulations, duties, and customs requirements.



H2A Agricultural Worker Program Bonds - Required for employers participating in the H2A temporary agricultural worker program.



HUD Manufactured Housing Installer Bonds - Ensure compliance with HUD installation standards for manufactured homes.

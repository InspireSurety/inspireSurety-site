What Is a HUD Bond?

A HUD bond (also called a HUD manufactured housing bond or HUD installer bond) is a federally required surety bond that ensures manufactured home installers, manufacturers, retailers, and related professionals comply with U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) regulations. This bond protects consumers and the federal government from financial loss caused by violations of HUD installation, construction, or safety standards.



A HUD bond protects the public from:



Improper installation of manufactured homes

Violations of HUD construction or safety codes

Failure to meet HUD program requirements

Financial harm caused by negligent or non‑compliant work

Misrepresentation or failure to follow federal guidelines

If you install, manufacture, or retail manufactured homes, HUD may require this bond before granting approval or certification.



HUD Bond Amounts and Cost

HUD bond amounts vary based on the type of HUD certification and typically range from $10,000 to $100,000+, depending on:



HUD program requirements

Whether you are an installer, manufacturer, or retailer

State‑specific HUD enforcement rules

Business size and volume

Who Needs a HUD Bond

You may need a HUD bond if you operate as a:



HUD‑certified manufactured home installer

Manufactured housing manufacturer

Manufactured home retailer or dealer

HUD contractor or subcontractor

Modular or mobile home service provider

Professional working under HUD installation standards

Any business involved in the installation or production of manufactured homes may be required to file this bond.



HUD Bond Requirements \& Eligibility

HUD typically requires:



Completed HUD certification or installer application

HUD‑mandated bond amount

Credit review

Business financials

Annual or multi‑year renewal

Some states require additional HUD‑related bonds depending on local enforcement rules.

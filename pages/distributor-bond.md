What Is a Distributor Bond?

&#x20;A distributor bond (also called a wholesale distributor bond or license \& permit distributor bond) is a state‑required surety bond that guarantees distributors comply with industry regulations, pay required taxes or fees, and operate ethically. This bond protects the public, suppliers, and state agencies from financial loss caused by violations of distributor laws.



A distributor bond protects against:



Failure to pay taxes, fees, or assessments

Misrepresentation of products

Violations of state distributor regulations

Failure to maintain accurate records

Non‑compliance with licensing requirements

If you’re applying for or renewing a distributor license—fuel, alcohol, tobacco, food, pharmaceuticals, or general wholesale—your state may require this bond before granting approval.

Fuel distributor or transporter

Any business requiring a state distributor license

Any company distributing regulated products may be required to file this bond.



Distributor Bond Requirements \& Eligibility

States typically require:



Completed distributor license application

State‑mandated bond amount

Credit review

Business financials

Annual or biannual renewal

Some industries—such as fuel, alcohol, and tobacco—may require higher bond amounts or additional documentation.

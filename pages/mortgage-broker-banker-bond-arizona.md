What Is an Arizona Mortgage Broker \& Mortgage Banker Bond?

An Arizona Mortgage Broker \& Mortgage Banker Bond is a state‑required surety bond that protects consumers and ensures compliance with Arizona lending laws. This includes:



Arizona Mortgage Broker Bond

Arizona Mortgage Banker Bond

Arizona Mortgage Loan Originator (MLO) Bond

Arizona Commercial Mortgage Broker Bond

These bonds guarantee that mortgage professionals follow Arizona Department of Insurance \& Financial Institutions (DIFI) regulations when originating, brokering, or servicing loans.

Who Needs an Arizona Mortgage Broker Bond?

You may need an Arizona mortgage bond if you operate as a:



Mortgage Broker

Mortgage Banker

Commercial Mortgage Broker

Mortgage Loan Originator (MLO)

If you originate, broker, or service residential or commercial mortgage loans, Arizona requires an active bond filed through NMLS.



Arizona Mortgage Broker Bond Requirements

Arizona mortgage licensing requirements typically include:



NMLS application

Fingerprints and background check

Financial statements

Required bond amount

Compliance with DIFI lending regulations

Mortgage bonds must remain active at all times to keep your license in good standing.



Arizona Mortgage Broker License

To obtain an Arizona mortgage broker or banker license, applicants must:



Apply through NMLS

Complete pre‑licensing education

Submit fingerprints

Provide financial documentation

Maintain the correct surety bond amount

Commercial mortgage brokers and bankers may have additional net‑worth requirements.



Arizona Surety Bond Requirements

Arizona assigns different bond amounts depending on:



License category

Annual loan production

Business type (broker, banker, MLO, commercial broker)

Bonds are filed electronically through NMLS and must stay active for the license to remain valid.

What Are License \& Permit Bonds?

License and permit bonds guarantee that a business will:



Follow state and local laws

Operate ethically

Pay required taxes, fees, or penalties

Protect consumers from financial harm

These bonds are required before a business can obtain or renew a license.



Industries That Commonly Require These Bonds

License and permit bonds apply to a wide range of regulated industries, including:



Auto dealers

Contractors

Mortgage brokers

Freight brokers

Notaries

Escrow agents

Collection agencies

Money Transmitters

Alcohol and tobacco sellers

Health Club Bonds

Each industry has its own bond requirements and regulations.





Key Takeaway -- License and permit bonds protect the public—not the business. If a violation occurs, the business must reimburse the surety for any valid claim.



How License \& Permit Bonds Work

License and permit bonds function like other surety bonds:



Business applies for the bond - The surety reviews credit, financials, and experience.

Bond is issued - The business pays a premium (typically 1–5%).

Business operates under the bond - They must follow all applicable laws and regulations.

If violations occur - A claim may be filed by a consumer or regulatory agency.

Surety resolves valid claims  - Then seeks reimbursement from the business.

These bonds protect the public — not the business.

Why License \& Permit Bonds Matter

These bonds:



Protect consumers from fraud or misconduct

Ensure businesses follow the law

Promote ethical business practices

Provide financial recourse for harmed parties

They are essential for maintaining trust in regulated industries.

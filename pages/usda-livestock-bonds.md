USDA Livestock Bonds
Get your USDA Livestock Bond.

A USDA Livestock Bond is required for livestock dealers, market agencies, and packers operating under the federal Packers and Stockyards Act (P&S Act). 

This bond guarantees that livestock sellers are paid promptly and that all transactions follow USDA‑mandated fair‑trade and financial‑responsibility standards. It protects producers, auction markets, and consignors if a dealer fails to pay or violates federal regulations.

Ensures full compliance with USDA Packers & Stockyards Act requirements for livestock dealers and market agencies.


Who Needs a USDA Livestock Bond
Livestock dealers
Market agencies (buying or selling on commission)
Packers purchasing livestock directly from producers
Any business required to register under the Packers and Stockyards Act
The USDA mandates this bond to ensure financial protection for livestock sellers and to maintain integrity in the national livestock market.

USDA Livestock Bond Amount
Bond amounts are set according to the dealer’s annual livestock purchase volume. Typical amounts range from $10,000 to $100,000+, depending on:

Annual livestock purchases
Type of operation (dealer, market agency, packer)
USDA risk assessment
Large‑volume operations may require significantly higher bond amounts.

USDA Livestock Bond Premium
Premiums are generally 1%–5% of the bond amount per year. Rates depend on:

Credit score
Financial strength
Business history
Purchase volume
Businesses with strong credit and stable financials qualify for the lowest premiums.

USDA Bond Requirements & Eligibility
USDA programs typically require:

Completed federal license application
USDA‑mandated bond amount
Credit review
Financial statements
Annual or semi‑annual renewal
Some USDA Livestock Bonds require increased bond amounts based on volume or risk.
